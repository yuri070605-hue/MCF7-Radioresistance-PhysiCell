About the Project

This project investigates how estrogen may influence tumor growth and radiotherapy response in three-dimensional estrogen receptor-positive MCF-7 breast cancer spheroids.

A three-dimensional agent-based computational model was developed using the open-source PhysiCell platform. The model incorporates cellular proliferation, apoptosis, oxygen diffusion, hypoxia, estrogen-dependent growth and radiation-induced cell death.

Main hypothesis: Estrogen promotes spheroid growth and may reduce radiotherapy effectiveness by increasing the viable tumor-cell population and contributing to hypoxia-mediated protection.

Methods

Computational model

  • Three-dimensional agent-based simulation using PhysiCell
  • Estrogen receptor-positive MCF-7 breast cancer cells
  • Cell proliferation and apoptosis
  • Oxygen diffusion and development of hypoxia
  • Estrogen-dependent regulation of proliferation
  • Linear-Quadratic model of radiation-induced cell death
  • Oxygen Enhancement Ratio to represent hypoxia-associated radioresistance

Simulated conditions

  1. Control
  2. Estrogen treatment
  3. Radiation treatment
  4. Estrogen plus radiation

Tumor growth was simulated for 14 days, with radiation delivered on Day 7.

Preliminary Simulation Results

Estrogen stimulation accelerated spheroid growth and increased the number of viable cells compared with the untreated control. Following radiation treatment, estrogen-treated spheroids showed a greater surviving cell population and faster post-treatment regrowth.

1,948 Control cells
6,274 Estrogen-treated cells
363 Radiation-treated cells
1,169 Estrogen + radiation cells
These preliminary findings suggest that estrogen may contribute to radioresistance through enhanced proliferation and hypoxia-associated protection. Experimental validation is required.

Limitations

  • The tumor microenvironment is represented using simplified assumptions.
  • Parameters were derived primarily from published literature.
  • Immune cells, stromal cells, angiogenesis and extracellular matrix remodeling were not included.
  • Intracellular estrogen receptor signaling was simplified.
  • The simulation results require experimental validation using three-dimensional MCF-7 spheroids.

Future Research

  • Validate the model using laboratory-grown MCF-7 spheroids.
  • Refine biological parameters using experimental measurements.
  • Include detailed estrogen receptor signaling pathways.
  • Model endocrine therapies such as tamoxifen and fulvestrant.
  • Investigate combined endocrine therapy and radiotherapy.
  • Incorporate immune, stromal and extracellular matrix components.
  • Develop patient-specific simulations for personalized treatment research.
